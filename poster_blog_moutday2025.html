Himalaya from Ancient Formation to an Uncertain Future

Mountain Building to Future Scenarios for People, Ecosystems, and Biodiversity
Geological Timeline
65-59 Million Years Ago
Initial India-Eurasia collision begins in central Himalayas, initiating continental convergence.
55-50 Million Years Ago
Collision progresses to western and eastern Himalayas. Ancient Tethys Ocean floor compressed.
40-30 Million Years Ago
Crustal shortening and thickening along Main Central Thrust. Early Himalayan peaks form.
20-10 Million Years Ago
Main Boundary Thrust becomes active. Deformation front migrates southward.
Present Day
Ongoing convergence at ~2cm/year. Elastic strain accumulates along Main Himalayan Thrust.
